# S155CP

## Register

product

## Users

Sniper 155 Club of the Philippines members and authorized club officers. Members primarily use the product on mobile to manage their club identity, browse member-only merchandise, place orders, submit payment proof, and track fulfillment. Officers use denser desktop workflows to manage membership, content, access, store configuration, payments, and orders.

## Product Purpose

Give S155CP one trusted member system for club operations and merchandise ordering. The product should make routine member actions quick, keep administrative work auditable, and preserve clear boundaries between member and officer access. Success means members can complete tasks without officer assistance while officers can operate the club from accurate, permission-controlled records.

## Brand Personality

Confident, disciplined, communal. Copy is direct and respectful, with pride in club membership but no manufactured bravado.

## Anti-references

- Generic public marketplace or SaaS dashboard styling that could belong to any organization.
- Chrome, flames, carbon-fiber textures, speed streaks, or other literal motorcycle clichés.
- Loud promotional layouts that compete with the member's current task.
- Ornamental motion, excessive gradients, glass effects, and novelty controls.

## Design Principles

- Put the current member or officer task first; navigation and decoration stay subordinate.
- Make access, state, deadlines, totals, and next actions explicit.
- Express club identity through disciplined consistency and authentic assets, not biker-themed ornament.
- Design mobile member workflows for quick use during rides and events; keep officer workflows efficient at desktop density.
- Prefer familiar, accessible controls and predictable feedback over novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Preserve keyboard access, visible focus, semantic structure, sufficient contrast, touch targets suitable for mobile use, reduced-motion preferences, and status cues that do not depend on color alone. Use plain language and keep critical actions legible in bright outdoor conditions.
